<div class = "lake">
 <h1>About <br> Kareri Lake </h1> 
</div>
<div class = "about_kareri">
Kareri Lake is high altitude, shallow, fresh water lake south of the Dhauladhar range approximately 9 km North West of Dharamshala in Kangra District, Himachal Pradesh. Its Surface is 2934 metres above the sea level. Snow melting from Dhauladhar range serves as the source of the lake and a stream nyund is the outflow. Since the Source is fresh melting snow and the lake is shallow, water visibility is very high and in most places the lake bed can be seen.
</div>
<div class = "k">
How To Reach Kareri Lake
</div>
<div class = "reri">
The best way to reach the Kareri Lake is to hire a jeep from Dharamshala to Ghera village, which is around 20 kilometres away from the Lake. From the main bazar, proceed towards the bridge, crossing the Bhote Khosi. after crossing the bridge, take a left and trek upwards, straight along the Kareri stream. The trail diverges here. stick to the onw that goes along the river. Around 30 minutes into the hike, the forested trail will lead you to a goverment school. This is Sari Village. The trek from here is another two kilometre-long ascent to the Kareri Village.

<div class = "About_john">
<h1> About <br> St. John  In The <br> Wilderness Church </h1>
</div>
<div class = "wilderness">
St. John in the Wilderness is a Church of North India church dedicated to John the Baptist Built in 1852, located near Dharamshala, India. Set amidst deodar forest, and built in neo-Gothic Architecture, the church is known for its Belgian stained-glass windows donated by Lady Elgin(Mary Louisa Lambton), Wife of Lord Elgin. Though the Church Structure Survived the 1905 Kangra Earthquake, which killed close to 19,800 people, imjured thousands in the Kangra area, and destroyed most buildings in Kangra, Mcleod Ganj and Dharamshala; its spire, Bell Tower, was however destroyed. Later a new bell, cast in 1915 by Mears and Stainbank, was brought from England and installed outside in the compound of the Church. 
</div>

<div class = "About_Indrahar">
<h1> About <br> Indrahar Pass </h1>
</div>
<div class = "Indrahar">
Indrahar Pass is a mountain pass in the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayas. Located at 32^017.825'N 76^0111.872'E an altitude of 4,342 metres (14,245 ft) above mean sea level, near the tourist town of Dharamshala in Himachal Pradesh, Indrahar Pass forms the border between Kangra and Chamba Districts. It is part of a very popular trekking route from Dharamshala. It attracts substantial tourist traffic during the trekking season between April-October.  
</div>
<div class = "how2">
How To Reach Indrahar Pass
</div>
<div class = "Reach2">
Wake up early in the morning and be ready to start your first day of trekking. Today you will trek for about 10 kilometres towards Triund through Dhramkot. And stay overnight in the Triund camp. After having breakfast; be ready to start trekking to  today's destination Lahesh Cave. To reach the Lahesh Cave, the troop will have to cross the overpass and hike on the blossoming ground, through the Conifer and oak trees outline. You will be staying tonight at the Lahesh Cave campsite. This is an exciting day, for which you have been waiting for. Today you will trek on the extensive snowfield of the Indrahar Pass. This day we will begin our trekking after the breakfast. Initially, we will start the uphill trek in the direction of Indrahar Pass, the trail is going to be a little difficult as there will a precipitous climb of 300 to 400 meters. This part of the trek will be demanding and tricky, but as you reach the pass you will forget all your efforts and be rewarded with magnificent vistas of the Pir Panjal Range (4,100 m) in the North direction and the Mani Mahesh Kailash peak (5656 Meters)in the East direction.  
</div>

<h1> About <br> Namgyal Monastery </h1>
</div>
<div class = "Namgyal">
Namgyal Monastery (also refered as "Dalai Lama's Temple") is currently located in Mcleod Ganj, Dharamshala, India. It is the personal Monastery of the 14th Dalai Lama. Another name for this temple-complex is Namgyal Tantric College. This Monastery's key role is to assist with rituals involving the Dalai Lama of Tibet. Its main tantric practices reportedly include those of Kalachakra, Yamantaka, Chakrasamvara, Guhyasamaja, and Vajrakilaya.

<div class = "Naddi">
Naddi is the village in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. It is located at an altitude of 2000 metres above sea level, in the upper reaches of the Kangra valley. The village is situated about 3 kilometers from Mcleod Ganj known world wide for the presence of the Dalai Lama. On 29 April 1959, the 14th Dalai Lama (Tenzin Gyatso) established the tibetan exile administration in the north Indian hill station of Mussoorie. Baijnath Temple is a Nagara style Hindu temple situated in a small town of Baijnath located in Kangra District, Himachal Pradesh Kangra India, and was construction in 1204A.D. by two local merchants named Ahuka and Manyuka. <h1> About <br> Masroor <br> Temple </h1>
</div>
<div class = "About_Masroor">
The Masroor Temples, also refered to as Masroor Temples or Rock-cut Temples at Masroor, is an early 8th-century complex of rock-cut hindu Temples in the Kangra valley. The temples face northeast, towards the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayas. They area version of North Indian Nagara architecture style, dedicated to Shiva, Vishnu Devi and Saura traditions of Hinduism, with its surviving iconography likely inspired by henotheistic framework. Though a major temples complex in the surviving form, the archeological studies suggest that the artists and architects had a far more ambititous plan and the complex remains incomplete. Much of the Masroor's temple's sculpture and reliefs have been lost.
</div>
